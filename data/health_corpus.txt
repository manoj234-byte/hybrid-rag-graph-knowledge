Diabetes mellitus is a chronic condition characterized by high blood sugar levels. Common symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss. Treatment options include lifestyle changes such as diet and exercise, oral medications like metformin, and insulin therapy.

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, increases the risk of heart disease and stroke. Symptoms can be silent, but severe cases may cause headaches and dizziness. Management involves medication such as ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, and lifestyle modifications.

Asthma is a respiratory condition causing airway inflammation and difficulty breathing. Triggers include allergens, exercise, and cold air. Treatments include inhaled corticosteroids and bronchodilators.

The flu (influenza) is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, and body aches. Vaccination is the best prevention method, and antiviral drugs can reduce severity.

Vitamin D deficiency can lead to bone disorders like rickets and osteomalacia. Sunlight exposure and dietary supplements are common prevention and treatment methods.

COVID-19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus and primarily affects the respiratory system. Symptoms range from mild cough and fever to severe pneumonia. Preventive measures include vaccination, mask-wearing, and social distancing.

Cancer refers to diseases characterized by uncontrolled cell growth. Treatment depends on cancer type and may include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and immunotherapy.

Cholesterol is a fatty substance essential for body function but high levels increase the risk of cardiovascular disease. Diet, exercise, and statin medications help manage cholesterol levels.

Mental health disorders such as depression and anxiety affect mood and behavior. Treatment options include psychotherapy, medication, and lifestyle changes.

Pregnancy involves physiological changes to support fetal development. Prenatal care includes nutritional support, regular check-ups, and screening for complications.

**--- 20 More Diseases ---**

1.  **Migraine** is a neurological condition characterized by severe headaches, often accompanied by throbbing pain, sensitivity to light and sound, nausea, and vomiting. Triggers can include stress, certain foods, and hormonal changes. Treatment involves pain relievers, triptans, and preventive medications like beta-blockers or CGRP inhibitors.

2.  **Osteoporosis** is a disease that weakens bones, making them brittle and prone to fractures. It often develops silently, with symptoms appearing only after a fracture. Risk factors include aging, hormone changes, and calcium deficiency. Treatment includes calcium and vitamin D supplements, bisphosphonates, and lifestyle changes.

3.  **Alzheimer's Disease** is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that causes brain cells to degenerate and die, leading to memory loss, cognitive decline, and behavioral changes. Its exact cause is unknown, but genetics and lifestyle factors play a role. Treatments aim to manage symptoms and include cholinesterase inhibitors and memantine.

4.  **Parkinson's Disease** is a progressive disorder of the nervous system affecting movement, characterized by tremors, stiffness, slow movement, and impaired balance. It results from a loss of dopamine-producing neurons. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms with medications like levodopa, dopamine agonists, and sometimes deep brain stimulation.

5.  **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)** involves the gradual loss of kidney function over time. Symptoms may include swelling, fatigue, and changes in urination. Causes include diabetes and high blood pressure. Treatment focuses on managing underlying conditions, diet modification, and dialysis or kidney transplant in advanced stages.

6.  **Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)** is a chronic autoimmune disorder causing inflammation of the joints, leading to pain, swelling, stiffness, and potential joint damage. It occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues. Treatment involves disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), biologics, and physical therapy.

7.  **Crohn's Disease** is a chronic inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) that can affect any part of the digestive tract, causing abdominal pain, severe diarrhea, fatigue, and weight loss. It is an autoimmune condition. Treatment includes anti-inflammatory drugs, immunosuppressants, biologics, and sometimes surgery.

8.  **Celiac Disease** is an autoimmune disorder where consuming gluten leads to damage in the small intestine, causing digestive symptoms like abdominal pain and bloating, and nutrient malabsorption. The only treatment is a strict, lifelong gluten-free diet.

9.  **Gout** is a form of arthritis characterized by sudden, severe attacks of pain, swelling, redness, and tenderness in the joints, most often the big toe. It is caused by a buildup of uric acid crystals in the joint. Treatment involves NSAIDs, colchicine, and medications to lower uric acid levels like allopurinol.

10. **Multiple Sclerosis (MS)** is a chronic, unpredictable disease affecting the central nervous system (brain, spinal cord, optic nerves). Symptoms vary widely but can include fatigue, numbness, vision problems, and difficulty walking. It is an autoimmune condition. Treatment involves disease-modifying therapies (DMTs) to slow progression and manage symptoms.

11. **Anemia** is a condition in which your blood lacks healthy red blood cells, leading to fatigue, weakness, pale skin, and shortness of breath. Common causes include iron deficiency, vitamin deficiencies, or chronic disease. Treatment depends on the cause but often involves supplements (e.g., iron, B12) or addressing underlying conditions.

12. **Hypothyroidism** is a condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormones, leading to symptoms like fatigue, weight gain, cold sensitivity, and dry skin. The most common cause is Hashimoto's disease. Treatment involves daily synthetic thyroid hormone medication (levothyroxine).

13. **Hyperthyroidism** occurs when the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone, causing symptoms like weight loss, rapid heartbeat, nervousness, and heat sensitivity. Graves' disease is a common cause. Treatment options include antithyroid medications, radioactive iodine therapy, or surgery.

14. **Cirrhosis** is a late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions, such as hepatitis and chronic alcoholism. Symptoms include fatigue, nausea, swelling in legs, and jaundice. Treatment focuses on addressing the underlying cause and managing complications; in severe cases, a liver transplant may be needed.

15. **Appendicitis** is an inflammation of the appendix, a finger-shaped pouch projecting from your colon. Symptoms typically include sudden pain that begins around the navel and shifts to the lower right abdomen, nausea, vomiting, and fever. Treatment almost always involves surgical removal of the appendix (appendectomy).

16. **Tuberculosis (TB)** is a serious infectious disease that primarily affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. It is caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Symptoms include persistent cough, fever, night sweats, and weight loss. Treatment involves a prolonged course of antibiotics.

17. **Dementia** is a general term for a decline in mental ability severe enough to interfere with daily life, encompassing various specific diseases like Alzheimer's. Symptoms include memory loss, difficulty with communication, and impaired reasoning. Management focuses on symptom relief, supportive care, and addressing any treatable underlying causes.

18. **Pneumonia** is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, which may fill with fluid or pus. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi. Symptoms include cough with phlegm, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. Treatment includes antibiotics (for bacterial), antiviral drugs (for viral), and supportive care.

19. **Gastric Ulcer** (Stomach Ulcer) is a sore that develops on the lining of the stomach. The most common symptoms are burning stomach pain. Causes include H. pylori infection and long-term use of NSAIDs. Treatment involves antibiotics (for H. pylori), proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), and avoiding irritants.

20. **Fibromyalgia** is a chronic disorder characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain accompanied by fatigue, sleep, memory, and mood issues. The cause is unknown but it's believed to amplify painful sensations by affecting the way your brain processes pain signals. Treatment involves pain relievers, antidepressants, exercise, and stress reduction techniques.
